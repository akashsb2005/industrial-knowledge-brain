THE FACTORIES ACT 1948 - KEY SAFETY PROVISIONS

SECTION 7A - GENERAL DUTIES OF OCCUPIER
Every occupier shall ensure:
- Plant and systems of work are safe and without risks to health
- Arrangements for safe use, handling, storage of substances
- Provision of information, instruction, training and supervision
- Maintenance of workplace in safe condition

SECTION 13 - VENTILATION
Every factory must be adequately ventilated with fresh air.
Confined spaces require forced ventilation before worker entry.
Gas concentration must be tested before confined space entry.

SECTION 21 - FENCING OF MACHINERY
All dangerous machinery must be securely fenced.
Moving parts of machinery must not be accessible during operation.
Maintenance on moving machinery strictly prohibited without lockout.

SECTION 36 - PRECAUTIONS AGAINST DANGEROUS FUMES
No person shall enter any chamber, tank, vat, pit, pipe, flue or other
confined space in any factory in which any gas, fume, vapour or dust
is likely to be present unless:
- Certificate of safety is issued by competent person
- Worker wears suitable breathing apparatus
- Standby person is present outside at all times
- Rescue equipment is readily available

SECTION 41B - SPECIFIC RESPONSIBILITIES OF OCCUPIER
- Maintain written records of all hazardous processes
- Appoint safety officers for factories with 1000+ workers
- Conduct health surveillance of workers in hazardous areas
- Submit annual safety audit report to inspector

PENALTIES:
- First violation: Up to 2 years imprisonment or Rs 2 lakh fine
- Repeat violation: Up to 3 years imprisonment or Rs 3 lakh fine
- Violation causing death: Up to 10 years imprisonment
