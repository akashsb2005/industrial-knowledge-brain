OISD STANDARD 105 - WORK PERMIT SYSTEM

1. INTRODUCTION
A Permit to Work (PTW) system is a formal written system used to control certain types of work that are identified as potentially hazardous. It is an essential part of a safe system of work for high-risk industries like oil, gas, and manufacturing.

2. TYPES OF WORK PERMITS
- Hot Work Permit: Required for any work involving open flame, welding, cutting, grinding
- Cold Work Permit: Required for non-spark generating maintenance activities
- Confined Space Entry Permit: Required before entering vessels, tanks, pits
- Electrical Work Permit: Required for work on live electrical equipment
- Height Work Permit: Required for work above 2 meters

3. PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
- All permits must be signed by the Area Authority before work begins
- Gas testing must be done before confined space entry
- Fire extinguisher must be present during hot work
- Permit must be displayed at work site at all times
- Permit validity maximum 8 hours per shift

4. RESPONSIBILITIES
- Issuing Authority: Ensures area is safe, signs the permit
- Performing Authority: Carries out the work safely
- Safety Officer: Monitors compliance during work execution

5. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
- Violation of PTW system is punishable under Factories Act Section 7A
- All permits must be archived for minimum 3 years
- Annual audit of PTW system is mandatory
- Non-compliance attracts penalties under OISD regulations

6. EQUIPMENT SAFETY
- Pump P-101: Requires cold work permit for maintenance
- Compressor C-201: Requires hot work permit for welding
- Vessel V-301: Requires confined space entry permit
- Bearing replacement interval: Every 8000 operating hours
- Seal replacement interval: Every 12000 operating hours
